{\textbf{Problem:}}\hspace{15}Find the direction in which a straight line must be drawn through the point  \myvec{-1\\2} so that its point of intersection with the line $$ (1 \hspace{10} 1)X = 4 $$ may be the distance of 3 units from this point.}
\newline
{\textbf{Solution:}}\newline Let$$ \boldsymbol \vec{A}=\myvec{3 \\ 1}, \boldsymbol \vec{m}=\myvec{1 \\ -1}$$ and $$ \boldsymbol \vec{B}=\myvec{-1 \\ 2}$$
Now, the given equation in parametric form is:
\begin{align}
     $$ \boldsymbol \vec {X} = \boldsymbol \vec{A}+\lambda \boldsymbol \vec{m} $$
\end{align}
 Given that the distance is 3 units.
 \begin{align}
     $$\implies \norm{\boldsymbol \vec{X}- \boldsymbol \vec{B}} =3 $$
 \end{align}
 \begin{align}
     $$\implies \lambda^2 + 5\lambda + 4 = 0 $$
 \end{align}
